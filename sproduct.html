Product Details

Collar: Cotton shirts often have a standard collar, such as a pointed collar, spread collar, or button-down collar. The collar design can change the shirt's formality level. Sleeves: They can have short or long sleeves. Long-sleeve shirts typically feature cuffs (with or without buttons), while short-sleeve versions are popular for warm weather. Fit: Cotton shirts come in different fits, including slim, regular, and relaxed fits. The cut of the shirt affects its overall appearance, with slim-fit shirts hugging the body more closely, while regular-fit shirts provide a more relaxed, roomy feel. Button Placket: A classic feature of most cotton shirts is the button placket that runs down the front of the shirt. The placket may feature a row of small, matching buttons, often made of plastic, mother-of-pearl, or wood. Some shirts have concealed buttons for a cleaner, dressier look. Hemline: The hem of the shirt can be straight, curved, or even scalloped. A curved hem is common for shirts designed to be worn untucked, while a straight hem is typically seen in more formal styles. Pockets: Many cotton shirts feature a single or double breast pocket, located on the chest area. These pockets can either be functional or purely decorative.
